Consequences of Climate Change

"Consequences of climate change." European Commission, climate.ec.europa.eu/climate-change/consequences-climate-change_en. Accessed 16 Sept. 2024.

Climate change Causes all sorts of natural disasters, such as flooding.

Flooding

Although rivers have flooded before climate change, the frequency and severity of the floods have increased, and are expected to increase more. An example of a recent severe flood is in Ahrtal in Germany in 2021, where 188 people lost their lives.

Droughts

Droughts are temporary water deficit in an area, and are caused by a lack of precipitation, and increased temperatures. They can stop crop growth, increase pest attacks, and increase wildfires.

Rising Sea Levels

Climate change also causes rising sea levels, which puts low-lying and island nations at risk. Additionally, it limits the availability of fresh water, as the sea can find its way into underground water tables and other fresh water sources.

Other Disasters

More severe storms (e.g. Hurricanes, Tornadoes, Thunderstorms) are also an effect of climate change, as it disrupts conventional weather systems.
